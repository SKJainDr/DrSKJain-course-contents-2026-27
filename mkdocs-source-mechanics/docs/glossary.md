# Glossary of Key Terms

Definitions of the recurring physical concepts used across the 18 experiments and 6 workshops in this guide, in alphabetical order. Each entry links back to the experiment(s) where the term is most central.

## A

**Adiabatic compressibility (β)** — The fractional decrease in volume per unit increase in pressure of a medium under conditions of no heat exchange; related to the velocity of sound by β = 1/(ρv²). See [Ultrasonic Interferometer](experiments/ultrasonic-interferometer.md).

**Angle of twist (θ)** — The angle through which one end of a wire rotates relative to the other under an applied torque; proportional to the applied couple for an elastic wire. See [Static Torsion](experiments/static-torsion.md).

## B

**Ballistic pendulum** — A device that determines a projectile's speed by catching it in a swinging pendulum block, combining momentum conservation (during the catch) with energy conservation (during the swing). See [Ballistic Pendulum](experiments/ballistic-pendulum.md).

**Beat period (T_beat)** — The period over which oscillatory energy visibly transfers back and forth between two weakly coupled oscillators with slightly different normal-mode frequencies. See [Coupled Pendulums](experiments/coupled-pendulums.md).

**Bulk modulus (K)** — A measure of a substance's resistance to uniform compression, K = ρv² for a fluid, where v is the speed of sound in it. See [Ultrasonic Interferometer](experiments/ultrasonic-interferometer.md).

## C

**Conjugate points** — A pair of pivot points on either side of the centre of gravity of a compound pendulum that give the same period of oscillation; the basis of Kater's reversible pendulum. See [Compound (Bar) Pendulum](experiments/compound-pendulum.md).

## D

**Damping ratio (ζ)** — A dimensionless measure of how strongly oscillations in a system are damped, relative to the critical damping value that just prevents oscillation altogether. See [Damped Oscillations](experiments/damped-oscillations.md), [Automotive Shock-Absorber Damping Design](workshops/shock-absorber-design.md).

## E

**Elastic limit** — The maximum stress a material can withstand while still returning completely to its original shape once the load is removed; beyond it, permanent (plastic) deformation occurs. See [Non-Uniform (Cantilever) Bending](experiments/non-uniform-bending.md), [Structural Beam Load Testing](workshops/beam-load-testing.md).

## F

**Frictional couple (τ_f)** — The torque exerted by friction at a rotating axle's bearings, opposing its rotation and dissipating its kinetic energy over time. See [Flywheel](experiments/flywheel.md).

## L

**Logarithmic decrement (δ)** — The natural logarithm of the ratio of two successive peak amplitudes in a damped oscillation, δ = ln(A_n/A_{n+1}); a direct, dimensionless measure of the damping strength. See [Damped Oscillations](experiments/damped-oscillations.md).

## M

**Maximum permissible error** — The upper bound on the uncertainty in a calculated result, obtained by propagating the least-count (instrument resolution) errors of the individually measured quantities through the governing formula.

**Moment of inertia (I)** — The rotational analogue of mass: a measure of a body's resistance to angular acceleration about a given axis, depending on both its mass and how that mass is distributed relative to the axis. See [Bifilar Suspension](experiments/bifilar-suspension.md), [Torsional Pendulum](experiments/torsional-pendulum.md), [Flywheel](experiments/flywheel.md).

## N

**Normal mode** — A pattern of motion in a coupled oscillating system in which every part of the system oscillates at the same single frequency; any general motion of the system can be written as a superposition of its normal modes. See [Coupled Pendulums](experiments/coupled-pendulums.md).

## P

**Poiseuille's law** — The relation giving the volume flow rate of a liquid through a narrow tube under laminar flow, proportional to the fourth power of the tube's radius. See [Poiseuille's Method](experiments/poiseuille-viscosity.md).

**Precession** — The slow, steady sweeping motion of a spinning body's rotation axis around another axis, caused by an applied torque that is perpendicular to its spin angular momentum. See [Gyroscopic Precession](experiments/gyroscopic-precession.md).

## Q

**Quality factor (Q)** — A dimensionless measure of how lightly damped an oscillator is; equivalently, the sharpness of its resonance peak, Q = f₀/Δf, or approximately π/δ from the decay of a free oscillation. See [Damped Oscillations](experiments/damped-oscillations.md), [Forced Oscillations & Resonance](experiments/forced-oscillations-resonance.md).

## R

**Radius of gyration (k)** — The distance from an axis at which the entire mass of a body could be concentrated to give the same moment of inertia about that axis. See [Compound (Bar) Pendulum](experiments/compound-pendulum.md).

**Resonance** — The dramatic increase in the steady-state amplitude of a driven oscillator when the driving frequency approaches its natural frequency. See [Forced Oscillations & Resonance](experiments/forced-oscillations-resonance.md).

**Reynolds number** — A dimensionless quantity that predicts whether flow will be laminar (low value) or turbulent (high value); Stokes' law and Poiseuille's law are both valid only at sufficiently low Reynolds number. See [Stokes' Method](experiments/stokes-viscosity.md), [Poiseuille's Method](experiments/poiseuille-viscosity.md).

**Rigidity (shear) modulus (η)** — The ratio of shear stress to shear strain for an elastic solid, characterising its resistance to twisting or shape-changing deformation at constant volume. See [Torsional Pendulum](experiments/torsional-pendulum.md), [Static Torsion](experiments/static-torsion.md).

**Rolling without slipping** — Rolling motion in which the contact point of a body has zero instantaneous velocity relative to the surface, so its linear speed and angular speed are locked together by v = rω. See [Rolling Race on an Incline](experiments/rolling-race-incline.md).

## S

**Stokes' law** — The formula for the viscous drag force on a small sphere moving slowly through a fluid, proportional to the sphere's radius, its speed, and the fluid's viscosity. See [Stokes' Method](experiments/stokes-viscosity.md).

**Surface tension (T)** — The tendency of a liquid surface to behave like a stretched elastic membrane, arising from unbalanced intermolecular attraction at the surface. See [Capillary Rise Method](experiments/capillary-rise.md).

## T

**Terminal velocity** — The constant speed reached by an object falling through a fluid once the net force on it (weight, buoyancy and drag combined) becomes zero. See [Stokes' Method](experiments/stokes-viscosity.md).

**Transmissibility** — The ratio of the force (or motion) transmitted through a vibration isolator to the force (or motion) generated by the source; the central design quantity in vibration isolation. See [Vibration Isolation & Machine Mounting](workshops/vibration-isolation.md).

## V

**Viscosity (η)** — A fluid's internal resistance to relative motion between its layers (internal friction), governing how readily it flows under an applied pressure or shear. See [Poiseuille's Method](experiments/poiseuille-viscosity.md), [Stokes' Method](experiments/stokes-viscosity.md).

## Y

**Young's modulus (Y)** — The ratio of longitudinal (tensile or compressive) stress to strain for an elastic solid, characterising its stiffness along the direction of an applied load. See [Non-Uniform Bending](experiments/non-uniform-bending.md), [Uniform Bending](experiments/uniform-bending.md), [Searle's Apparatus](experiments/searle-apparatus.md).
